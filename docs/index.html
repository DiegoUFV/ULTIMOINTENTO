Smart Grids

November 9, 2023
7.85/10

Smart grids are a network of electricity that uses digital technologies like advanced metering infrastructure (AMI), smart distribution management systems (SDMS), and smart grid communication networks among others to monitor and manage the transportation of electricity from the generation sources to meet the needs of the users. Their usage varies from the supply of electricity to consumers using digital communications, and for the better optimization of this process the IoT capabilities are required. One of the many advantages that IoT brings to smart grids is the automatization of resource usage to minimize power and water consumption of businesses. All of this offers a new horizon in power generation, power delivery, reducing the number of cuts and decreasing the bill costs overall. It also has functionality when using it for deep-learning applications, as IoT brings vast amounts of data, this helps to model and give information with the help of smart grids to sort out high demand responses in peak hour situations. With the IoT and deep learning implementations we could be able to predict and learn peak hours for high demands of electricity and thus making the processes much more responsive and efficient and make our electricity waste much lower and our efficiency much greater.

Given the evolution that IoT has had in recent years, we can predict that its evolution is far from over.
